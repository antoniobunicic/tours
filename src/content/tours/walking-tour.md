Step into nearly two millennia of history as our expert local guides lead you through the living labyrinth of Split's UNESCO-listed Diocletian's Palace. Built by the Roman Emperor Diocletian in 305 AD, this extraordinary complex is not a museum — it's a living neighbourhood where people have called the ancient walls home for over a thousand years.

Starting at the Golden Gate, you'll wind through marble-paved streets lined with Roman columns, Byzantine chapels, and Renaissance palaces layered on top of one another. You'll pause in hidden courtyards where locals sip coffee in the shadow of 4th-century stonework, and descend into the atmospheric palace basement — the best-preserved Roman substructure in the world.

Our guides are born-and-raised Split locals who share authentic stories passed down through their families — from the Roman emperor who chose this stretch of Dalmatian coastline for his retirement, to the medieval refugees who turned imperial halls into apartment buildings, to the vibrant cultural life that thrives within the walls today.

Whether you're a history enthusiast, a photography lover, or simply want to experience the city like a local, this tour offers an unforgettable window into the true character of Split.
